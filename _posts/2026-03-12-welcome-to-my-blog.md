## Introduction

Welcome to my blog.

I am a **Red Teamer and Security Researcher** passionate about understanding how systems break and how they can be secured better. This blog serves as a personal space where I document my experiments, research, tools, and lessons learned while exploring the offensive side of cybersecurity.

The idea behind starting this blog is simple:

> Learn in public, share knowledge, and contribute to the security community.

Cybersecurity is a field where continuous learning is essential. Documenting research not only helps others but also helps me track my own progress and refine my understanding.

---

## What This Blog Will Cover

This blog will primarily focus on topics related to offensive security and technical research, including:

- Red Team operations and methodologies
- Vulnerability research
- Exploitation techniques
- Security tools and automation
- Capture The Flag (CTF) writeups
- Linux and Active Directory privilege escalation
- Malware and reverse engineering basics

Most posts will be **technical deep dives** based on hands-on experimentation.

---

## Why I Started This Blog

One of the best ways to improve in cybersecurity is by explaining concepts clearly and documenting findings.

Many excellent security researchers share their work publicly, and their blogs have helped countless learners. This blog is my way of contributing back to that ecosystem.

My goals for this blog are:

- Document technical research
- Share practical knowledge
- Improve my technical writing
- Build a personal archive of experiments

---

## My Approach to Security Research

My approach to learning cybersecurity is heavily **hands-on**. Instead of relying only on theory, I prefer:

- Breaking things in lab environments
- Studying real-world vulnerabilities
- Building and testing tools
- Understanding attacker techniques

Red teaming is particularly interesting to me because it simulates real adversaries and reveals weaknesses that traditional security assessments sometimes miss.
